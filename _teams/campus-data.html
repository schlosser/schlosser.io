In the summer of 2013, I joined several other college students or recent graduates at the Campus Data Summit to discuss how we can open up University dataset for student developers. I was joined by students from University of Waterloo, University of Pennsylvania, Rutgers University, Carnegie Mellon University, and Duke University. At this event, we wrote the original version of campusdata.org, a resource for student open data activists. We also wrote the Campus Data Guidebook, which collected stories and advice from our members.

Three years later, I built a new version of the Campus Data website, which features a new page showing rankings of twenty schools and which data sets they have exposed to their students.
